About

Kamal Haasan is an Indian film actor, director, screenwriter, producer, and politician. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest actors in Indian cinema. With a career spanning more than six decades, he has made significant contributions to the Indian film industry and has been honored with several awards.

Haasan is known for his versatility, having acted in multiple languages, including Tamil, Hindi, Telugu, Kannada, and Malayalam. His performances have ranged from romantic roles to intense character-driven portrayals. He has also been recognized for his innovative filmmaking and his involvement in various social and political causes.

Career

Kamal Haasan started his film career at a very young age, appearing as a child artist in the 1959 film "Kalathur Kannamma." His breakthrough role as an adult came with the film "Apoorva Raagangal" in 1975. Over the decades, he has become a household name in India, particularly in the Tamil film industry.

Haasan is known for his willingness to experiment with various roles, often opting for characters that challenge conventional norms. His films like "Nayakan" (1987), "Indian" (1996), and "Vishwaroopam" (2013) have been widely celebrated, and he is considered a pioneer in Indian cinema for his technical innovation and performance artistry.

Besides acting, Kamal Haasan has directed, produced, and written several films. He is known for his association with some of the most prestigious cinematic ventures in India, and his collaborations with other greats of the industry, such as directors like Mani Ratnam and Shankar, have been immensely popular.

Awards and Recognition

Kamal Haasan has received numerous prestigious awards throughout his career. He has been honored with the National Film Award multiple times for his outstanding performances and contributions to Indian cinema. In addition, he has received the Padma Bhushan (2000), one of India's highest civilian honors, in recognition of his immense contribution to the arts.

He has also received numerous state awards, including Tamil Nadu State Film Awards and Filmfare Awards South. His iconic roles in films like "Nayakan," which was India’s official entry for the Oscars, and "Indian" have earned him international recognition as well.

  • Padma Bhushan – 2000
  • National Film Award for Best Actor – Multiple times
  • Filmfare Award for Best Actor – South (Various Years)
  • Tamil Nadu State Film Award for Best Actor – Multiple times
  • Filmfare Lifetime Achievement Award
  • Special Jury Award at the Cannes Film Festival for "Thevar Magan"
